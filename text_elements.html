Text Elements in HTML

Headings

H1 headings should be the most important element on a page: the header. There are lower levels of headings including H2, H3, H4, H5, and H6. These other headings used be used for content on smaller sections of the page.

The Strong Element

The strong element is used to semantically place a hard emphasis on something important within a piece of text. This allows a screen reader to read the text with the proper emphasis and highlight important information.

While the strong element may look similar to using bold, they are not used in the same way; strong elements are used for proper communication of a text while bold is just purely aesthetics.

The Em Element

The em element functions in a similar way to the strong element in the sense that they both semantically place emphasis on a piece of content. While the em element may look like italics, they do not function in the same way as mentioned above. Italics are purely aesthetic, while the em element emphasizes a selection of text so a screen reader can properly read the piece of text in the way it's meant to be read.
